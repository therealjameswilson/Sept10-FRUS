# Compiler Gap Assessment

Prepared May 23, 2026.

This is a compiler-risk review of the current source-lead set for a prospective
FRUS-style volume on counterterrorism from January 20 to September 10, 2001. The
site is useful as a source-control workbench, but it is not yet ready to be
treated as a defensible document selection.

## Compiler Risk Judgment

The present collection should be treated as a map of leads, not a draft volume.
The highest-risk problem is that the central policy, warning, and operational
records are often represented by Commission citations, later investigations,
finding aids, memoirs, or source shelves rather than the underlying original
records.

## Fatal Gaps Before Selection

These gaps would create the most serious compiler risk if the chronology were
presented as a selected volume now.

### 1. NSC Decision Records

The core NSC process is still mostly represented by citation leads. Retrieve or
verify the original decision papers, minutes, summaries, and distribution records
for:

- March 7 informal Deputies materials, including John Bellinger notes and CIA
  briefing materials.
- March 2001 draft Afghanistan counterterrorism finding and draft UBL MON.
- April 30 Deputies Committee al Qaeda policy meeting Summary of Conclusions.
- June 7 Hadley memorandum, "Next Steps on al-Qida."
- June 29 Deputies Summary of Conclusions and the Rocca-to-Grossman briefing
  memo.
- July 11 Hadley memorandum on Predator.
- August 1-2 armed Predator Deputies materials.
- September 4 Principals Committee approval of the draft al Qaeda directive.
- September 10 Deputies Committee meeting on implementation.
- September 10 Hadley-to-Tenet covert-authorities memorandum.
- The pre-September 11 draft lineage that became NSPD-9.

Risk if unresolved: the volume would describe the policy process without the
documents that actually carried decisions, options, objections, taskings, and
clearances.

### 2. CIA, PDB, And DCI Threat Stream

The warning surge is the heart of the volume, but much of it is still visible
only through the 9/11 Commission narrative and notes. Retrieve or account for:

- May 29 warning item, "Bin Ladin network's plans advancing."
- June threat-reporting series behind the late-June "system blinking red"
  discussions.
- Tenet-Rice late-June meeting records, including date, participants, briefing
  papers, and whether the meeting was June 28 or another late-June date.
- PDB and SEIB items in the May-August threat spike beyond the released August 6
  PDB.
- August 23 DCI update item, "Islamic Extremist Learns to Fly."
- August 24 CIA suspicious 747 flight-training cable.

Risk if unresolved: warning chronology precision would rest on retrospective
summaries rather than the intelligence products and briefing records that shaped
official knowledge.

### 3. CSG And Domestic Warning Records

The Counterterrorism Security Group track is a repeated hinge between foreign
intelligence and domestic agency action. Retrieve or account for:

- April 19 CSG materials on Abu Zubaydah reporting.
- July 5 CSG meeting with domestic security agencies.
- July 6 CSG materials, if distinct from the July 5 domestic-agency meeting.
- July 23 CSG item on al Qaeda threat and suspected terrorist travel.
- July 2 FBI/FAA warning-action records.
- July 9 federal-building-security committee materials.
- Distribution lists showing what agencies received specific warnings.

Risk if unresolved: the volume would under-document how warning information moved
from the NSC process to domestic aviation, law-enforcement, border, and building
security channels.

### 4. FBI, FAA, Border, And Watchlisting Originals

Several high-value operational leads are still anchored in later DOJ OIG, Joint
Inquiry, FBI Review Commission, or Commission accounts. Retrieve or verify:

- The original Phoenix Electronic Communication and its routing.
- Moussaoui case-opening records, FISA discussions, field-headquarters messages,
  and intelligence-sharing records.
- CIA watchlisting cable for Khalid al-Mihdhar and Nawaf al-Hazmi.
- August 28 FBI New York lead to locate Khalid al-Mihdhar.
- FAA June 22, July 18, and July 31 circular originals, not only comparison PDFs
  or later summaries.
- INS, Customs, State visa, and border records connected to Hazmi, Mihdhar, and
  suspected terrorist travel.

Risk if unresolved: the record set would conflate investigative interpretations
with contemporaneous operational documents.

### 5. Diplomatic Pressure Track

State FOIA has provided several strong records, but important diplomatic pressure
documents remain missing or indirect. Retrieve or verify:

- State 111711, "Demarche on Threat by Afghan-based Terrorists," June 27, 2001.
- June 28 Rocca-to-Grossman memorandum for the June 29 Deputies meeting.
- White House letter from President Bush to President Musharraf, August 4, 2001.
- Pakistan, Taliban, and Afghanistan demarche traffic around June-August 2001.
- The exact July 18 Arabian Peninsula public warning or source cable.

Risk if unresolved: the selection would be too Washington-process heavy and would
not adequately document foreign-government pressure and notification.

### 6. September 10 Endpoint

The September 10 endpoint needs unusually careful control because it is easy to
blur pre-attack policy records with September 11 or post-attack retrospective
materials. Retrieve or verify:

- September 10 Deputies Committee Summary of Conclusions.
- September 10 Hadley-to-Tenet covert-authorities memorandum.
- September 10 Ashcroft-to-Daniels budget letter.
- September 10 State INR Massoud assessment, already represented by a State FOIA
  PDF but still requiring final annotation.
- September 10 Air Force/Vigilant Guardian records as endpoint context only, not
  a substitute for NSC policy records.

Risk if unresolved: the final lane would mix decision records, intelligence
context, air-defense exercise context, and retrospective significance without
clear source boundaries.

## Scope And Evidence Controls

- Memoirs by Bush, Cheney, and Rice are useful for recollection context and
  annotation. They should not be used as substitute records for meetings,
  taskings, or decisions.
- Bush 41 public statements are a reference shelf for counterterrorism language
  and inherited public-policy vocabulary. They are outside the January
  20-September 10, 2001 document-selection scope.
- ISCAP, Joint Inquiry, DOJ OIG, FBI Review Commission, and 9/11 Commission
  materials are source shelves and retrieval maps unless they reproduce or link
  the original contemporaneous record.
- Source collections should not be given false chronology precision. If a
  collection is anchored to a representative date, label it as a shelf rather
  than a dated event.
- Citation PDFs should stay visually and editorially distinct from direct
  declassified PDFs.

## Site/Data Gaps To Close

- Add a `retrievalPriority` or `compilerRisk` field so the P0/P1/P2 distinction
  is machine-readable.
- Add a `scopeRole` field for `direct-document`, `citation-lead`,
  `source-shelf`, `context-only`, and `reference-only`.
- Add a field separating `directPdf` from `citationPdf` for every record.
- Track exact archival target fields: repository, collection, file unit, title,
  date, page span, classification markings, release authority, and excisions.
- Split records that currently combine an event with a source shelf.
- Add a "not public yet" marker distinct from "not searched."

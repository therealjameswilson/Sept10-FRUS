# FBI 9/11 Review Commission Chronology

Date prepared: 2026-05-21

Source: Federal Bureau of Investigation, `The FBI: Protecting the Homeland in the 21st Century`, Report of the Congressionally-directed 9/11 Review Commission to the Director of the Federal Bureau of Investigation, March 2015: <https://www.fbi.gov/file-repository/reports-and-publications/final-9-11-review-commission-report-unclassified.pdf>

Compiler note: This chronology tracks events, meetings, briefings, interviews, and documents that the report refers to. It is a source-control chronology, not a finding that every item should become a FRUS document candidate. Chapter V is the most directly relevant section for the January 20-September 10, 2001 scope; later review materials are useful for annotation and source tracing.

## Selection Guidance

- Treat Chapter V as a source-discovery tool. It points to FBI files, summaries, MFRs, affidavits, briefings, and interviews; it is not a substitute for those underlying records.
- The best retrieval targets are the April 25, 2014 `Overview of 9/11 Investigation` briefing; the April 30, 2014 Sarasota MFR; the October 24, 2014 Guantanamo trial-preparation MFR; the November 10, 2014 Bassem Youssef MFR; the July 2004 al-Bayoumi summary; and the 2012 FBI summary on al-Thumairy.
- For the January 20-September 10, 2001 volume scope, the strongest contemporaneous leads remain al-Hazmi/al-Mihdhar domestic travel and assistance, Abdullah late-August speculation, and any pre-September 11 FBI/INS/State/CIA records that connect to those trails. Later claims should be used mainly to identify records and annotate uncertainty.

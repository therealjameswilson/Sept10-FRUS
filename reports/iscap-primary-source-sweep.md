# ISCAP Primary Source Sweep

Target searched: <https://www.archives.gov/declassification/iscap/releases>

This sweep focused on National Archives ISCAP releases that can strengthen the
pre-September 11 counterterrorism source map. The main gain is not volume-ready
selection by itself; it is a set of official NARA release pages and PDFs that
turn several weak citation leads into firmer primary-source trails.

## Also Noted

- [Prince Turki interview, ISCAP 2019-060](https://www.archives.gov/files/declassification/iscap/pdf/2019-060-doc-1.pdf): useful for background annotation on Saudi, Taliban, and UBL issues, especially rendition efforts and Taliban relations. It is not a contemporaneous 2001 U.S. policy record.
- [Terrorist Attack Incident Summary and Updates and Logs, ISCAP 2012-156](https://www.archives.gov/declassification/iscap/pdf/2012-156): strong post-attack incident-log collection, but outside the September 10 cutoff except as a boundary/reference source.
- [Rumsfeld memorandum on October 2001 regional visits, ISCAP 2012-158-doc1](https://www.archives.gov/files/declassification/iscap/pdf/2012-158-doc1.pdf): valuable for post-attack diplomacy, but outside this volume's pre-attack scope.
